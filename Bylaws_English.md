**Article 2 –** The purposes of the institution (three areas) are as follows:

1. Advancing scientific research in the fields of soil science, soil classification, soil pollution, soil fertility, sustainable soil management, and all related disciplines.
2. Transferring knowledge through conferences, workshops, forums, and social media, and publishing peer-reviewed research.
3. Providing scientific consultations to governmental and public/private agricultural entities to support environmental and agricultural policies.

## Chapter Two
### Board of Trustees

**Article 4 – Board of Trustees:**

1. The institution shall have a Board of Trustees composed of ...... trustees (at least 5, and an odd number). The first Board of Trustees shall consist of the founders named in the founding deed, and the Board shall be chaired by Mr. .............., acting as Chairman of the institution.
2. The Chairman of the Board of Trustees may not relinquish the chairmanship of the institution to a person of his choosing, nor appoint a successor; a new chairman for the institution, with full powers, must instead be elected.
3. If the position of Chairman of the institution becomes vacant without a successor having been appointed by him, the Board of Trustees shall elect a new chairman for the institution, with full powers.

**Article 5 – Appointment:**

1. The remaining trustees shall be appointed based on their ability to contribute to achieving the institution's objectives, for a term of two years, by decision of the Chairman of the institution.
2. The Board of Trustees may renew a trustee's membership after a one-term break, once every three terms.
3. Each trustee on the Board has one vote.
4. If a seat on the Board of Trustees becomes vacant for any reason, the Chairman of the institution may appoint a replacement for the remainder of the Board's term.

**Article 6 – Membership Conditions and Rights:**

1. **Conditions required of a member of the institution:**
   1. Must not be deprived of the exercise of their political rights.
   2. Must be of good conduct and reputation.
   3. Must have accepted the institution's bylaws in writing.
   4. Must be over eighteen years of age.
   5. Must reside in the Syrian Arab Republic.
   6. Must not have been convicted of a felony or misdemeanor.
   7. Must not be related to any other member of the Board of Trustees within the first or second degree of kinship.
   8. Must not be a member of any other association, institution, volunteer team, or other entity working in the humanitarian or charitable field.

2. **Rights of members of the institution:**
   1. Participation in setting the institution's general policy.
   2. Voting on the institution's decisions.
   3. Withdrawal from the institution by resignation approved by the Chairman of the Board of Trustees.
   4. Access to the institution's financial and administrative reports.
   5. Submitting proposals.

**Article 7 – Termination of Membership:**

Membership on the Board of Trustees ends in the following cases:

1. Resignation, by a letter submitted by the member to the Chairman of the Board of Trustees; the resignation, once accepted by the Chairman of the institution, becomes effective as of the date it was submitted.
2. If the member commits a disgraceful act that causes material, moral, or reputational harm to the institution, the member shall be dismissed by decision of the Chairman of the institution, after consulting the Board of Trustees.
3. If the member exploits their Board membership for personal gain, or no longer meets a membership condition, the member shall be dismissed by decision of the Chairman of the institution, after consulting the Board of Trustees.
4. Any Board member who is absent from three consecutive Board meetings without a legitimate excuse may be considered to have withdrawn.
5. Death.

**Article 8 – Powers and Duties of the Board of Trustees:**

The Board of Trustees shall undertake the following duties and powers:

1. Setting the institution's policies and determining the means to achieve its objectives.
2. Amending the institution's bylaws in accordance with the provisions set out in the bylaws.
3. Approving the closing accounts for the past fiscal year and providing a copy to the Ministry.
4. Approving the institution's annual report on its activities and status during the past year.
5. Approving the institution's estimated budget for the coming fiscal year.
6. Appointing an external auditor for the institution, from outside the Board of Trustees, and approving the auditor's reports.
7. Selecting and appointing the director of the institution's executive and administrative body.
8. Deciding to establish branches of the institution.
9. Securing the resources necessary for the institution to carry out its activities and achieve its objectives.

**Article 9 – Meetings of the Board of Trustees:**

1. Board meetings shall be held at the institution's headquarters or at any location specified in the meeting invitation.
2. The Board shall hold regular meetings, the dates of which are set by decision of the Chairman, provided there are no fewer than three meetings per year.
3. The Board may be called to an extraordinary meeting at any time at the invitation of the Chairman or one-third of its members.
4. The Secretary shall send invitations to trustees ten days before the meeting date, specifying in the invitation the purpose, place, date, and agenda of the meeting; notification may be made by any written, electronic, or other means of communication.
5. A Board meeting shall not be considered legally valid unless attended by more than half of the Board's members.
6. The Board of Trustees may invite whomever it deems appropriate to attend its meetings.

**Article 10 – Voting and Decision-Making:**

1. The institution's decisions are made by the Chairman of the Board of Trustees, after consulting the opinions of the Board members.
2. A Board member may not participate in discussion of a decision if the matter under discussion relates to a personal interest of the member or of someone the member represents.

**Article 11 – Compensation:**

- A member of the Board of Trustees may not work for the institution for pay.
- A member of the Board of Trustees is entitled to reimbursement of expenses incurred in the course of their duties, such as travel expenses and others.

**Article 12 – Duties of the Chairman of the Board of Trustees:**

The Chairman of the Board of Trustees has full authority to carry out the following:

1. Represent the institution before third parties and sign on its behalf before all government and private departments of every kind, in connection with all administrative, financial, and legal matters, including representation before the courts, and matters of delegation, power of attorney, and instruments arising from obligations.
2. Supervise all of the institution's work and oversee the proper conduct of its activities.
3. Act as the institution's authorizing and spending officer, with authority to move funds and open bank accounts in the institution's name.
4. Have the right to acknowledge claims, settle disputes, waive rights, and sell or mortgage the institution's assets, after consulting the Board of Trustees.
5. Hold signing authority on behalf of the institution, with the Chairman's signature being binding on the institution.
6. Appoint staff and managers and form the committees necessary for the institution's work.

**Article 13 – Duties of the Vice-Chairman of the Board of Trustees:**

The Vice-Chairman shall perform the duties of the Chairman in the Chairman's absence, and shall carry out any task assigned by the Chairman of the Board of Trustees.

**Article 14 – Duties of the Secretary of the Board of Trustees:**

The Secretary shall perform the following duties:

1. Record and keep the minutes of meetings, including the date and time of the meeting, the names of the trustees invited, the names of those present, the decisions issued during the meeting, and the number of votes for and against each decision.
2. Draft the invitations to Board meetings.
3. Keep the records of Board meeting minutes and the institution's official seals.

**Article 15 – Duties of the Treasurer of the Board of Trustees:**

The Treasurer shall perform the following duties:

1. Receive funds coming to the institution against receipts bearing the institution's seal and the Treasurer's signature, and deposit such funds in a bank accepted by the Ministry of Social Affairs and Labor.
2. Make payments against a payment order signed by the Treasurer and by the Chairman of the Board of Trustees or the Vice-Chairman.
3. Withdraw funds from the bank jointly with the authorizing officer.
4. Implement the Board of Trustees' financial decisions.
5. Keep the institution's financial ledgers and documents at its headquarters.

## Chapter Three
### Administration of the Institution

**Article 16 – Administration of the Institution:**

1. The Board of Trustees shall be assisted in administering the institution by an executive body comprising the administrative, legal, and financial expertise needed to implement the Board's policies and achieve the institution's objectives.
2. The executive body shall be headed by an Executive Director appointed by the Board of Trustees, who shall hold full powers and authority to manage the institution under a delegation granted by the Board of Trustees.
3. The Executive Director shall be accountable to the Board of Trustees and to third parties for any violation committed of the decisions issued by the Board of Trustees, the institution's bylaws, and the provisions of the Law on Private Associations and Institutions No. 93 of 1958, including the founding deed and bylaws of the institution, as well as the decisions issued by the Board of Trustees.

**Article 17 – Duties of the Executive Director:**

The Executive Director shall perform all duties that ensure the institution runs smoothly, including contributing to the fulfillment of the institution's purpose and objectives, as follows:

1. Supervise the work of the institution's executive body.
2. Propose names of candidates for the positions of assistant directors.
3. Supervise the preparation of the institution's financial and administrative systems, in cooperation with the relevant assistant directors.
4. Prepare the annual report on the institution's activities.
5. Prepare the draft estimated budget for the coming year and the closing accounts for the past fiscal year, in cooperation with the executive body's financial director.
6. Approve the internal auditor's report on the institution's accounts.

## Chapter Four
### Institution Finances

**Article 18 – Financial Resources of the Institution:**

The institution's financial resources consist of:

1. Donations received by the institution.
2. Gifts, bequests, and grants.
3. Other resources approved by the Board of Trustees after notifying the Ministry in accordance with the law.
4. Foreign aid, subject to the approval of the Ministry of Social Affairs and Labor.
5. Savings and internal or external borrowing, provided that the approval of the Ministry of Social Affairs and Labor is obtained in the case of external borrowing.
6. Income and profits generated by the institution's investment activities.
7. The institution may increase its resources through safe investments, after consulting the Board of Trustees.

**Article 19 – Use of Funding Sources:**

The institution's financial resources shall be used in a manner that contributes to achieving the purpose for which it was established, and may not be used for any purpose that would hinder the achievement of the objectives set out in the founding deed and bylaws of the institution.

**Article 20 – Fiscal Year:**

1. The institution's fiscal year begins on the first of January and ends on the last day of December of each year.
2. The institution shall keep its financial records and books at its headquarters.
3. When establishing a full accounting unit, the accountant must be from the same competent authority.
4. Compliance with the salary scale set by the relevant Ministry or competent authority.
5. Compliance with the work protocols and standard regulations adopted by the competent authorities.

## Chapter Six
### Branches of the Institution

**Article 21 –** The institution may establish branches for itself. Such branches shall not have legal personality unless duly registered/publicized. The right to establish unregistered branches is limited to the institution's area of activity as defined in these bylaws. An unregistered branch shall be established by decision of the Board of Trustees after the Ministry's approval, and the branch's administrative committee shall likewise be appointed by decision of the Board of Trustees.

**Article 22 –** Each branch of the institution, in its composition and operations, is subject to the provisions of these bylaws, and a branch may not amend its regulations regarding its obligations, general policy, or purposes except with the institution's approval.

**Article 23 –** Each branch must submit an annual report on its activities and financial status.

**Article 24 –** Upon the dissolution of any branch of the institution, its funds shall revert to the institution.

## Chapter Seven
### Dissolution of the Institution

**Article 25 –** The founding deed is considered an integral part of the institution's bylaws.

**Article 26 –** The bylaws may be amended with the approval of a majority of the members of the Board of Trustees, provided this does not conflict with the laws governing the operation of private associations and institutions.

**Article 27 –** The Ministry of Social Affairs and Labor shall appoint a legal liquidator to liquidate the institution's funds and assets and settle its obligations.

**Article 28 –** Upon the dissolution of the institution for any reason, the Ministry of Social Affairs and Labor shall take over the administration of all of the institution's movable and immovable assets and funds and shall use them for humanitarian and charitable purposes.

**Article 29 –** The institution has the right to add new articles to its internal bylaws after obtaining the Ministry's approval.

**Article 30 –** If the Board of Trustees determines that the institution has become unable to achieve its purposes, it may call a meeting to consider the institution's dissolution; the institution may not be dissolved except by a decision taken by a majority of at least two-thirds of its members.
